Batman Arkham Knight reportedly broken on pc

Batman Arkham Knight reportedly broken on pc

Tweet

Batman Arkham Knight was just released a few hours ago and it is not off to a good start, there have been many reports from pc gamers that claim this game is literally unplayable at the moment. These claims are coming from numerous customers on steam complaining about the game being poorly optimised with extremely bad stuttering especially when you try summon the Batmobile, horrendous soundbugs and the game being locked at 30fps. Users have found a way to unlock the 30fps cap by changing .ini file settings with some claiming increasing the fps helps relieve stuttering (except when summoning batmobile) and other saying it makes stuttering less frequent but more noticeable. Games with buggy releases are not uncommon and depending on the size of the game it is practically unavoidable for a game development team to release a game without any bugs, but if Batmans performance issues are as bad as people are claiming why on earth release it, even from an outside stand point it looks and sounds like another game badly ported to pc and shoved out the door before it was ready.

Luckily though Rocksteady Studios have acknowledged the problem and claim "We're aware that some users are reporting performance issues with the PC version of Batman: Arkham Knight. This is something that Rocksteady takes very seriously. We are working closely with our external PC development partner to make sure these issues get resolved as quickly as possible. We'll update this thread when we've got more info to share.". hopefully Rocksteady Studios can redeem themselves and release a patch soon before pc gamers get too frustrated and loose interest.
