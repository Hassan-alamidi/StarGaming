Bethesda SoftWorks E3 Press conference on Doom 4

Doom 4 E3

Tweet

No more than a few weeks ago Bethesda released a short but sweet trailer for Doom 4, this trailer did not give us much but it let us know it was coming, and as promised the Doom 4 got a full reveal at E3. As explained by Software executive producer Marty Stratton "As you now Find yourself on the outskirts of a massive UAC research facility on Mars you have been activated to do one thing kill demons", the conference then goes on to show actual gameplay of Doom 4.

The gameplay demo shown at E3 for Doom 4's single player looked amazing, it was fast paced action soaked in demon blood and guts. I was honestly really impressed with this game the graphics look decent, the enemy and player animations looked outstanding, the kill moves looked horrifyingly satisfying and the gameplay itself looked incredibly fun and exciting (I could feel the adrenalin just by watching it).

Dooms Multi-Player in terms of combat seems to contain all the single player goodness but faster paced and with friends. The Multi-player Maps vary from "UAC research facility's on Mars to the depths of hell", the game modes we know about so far right off the bat are domination, Freeze tag (I am sure we can assume this game mode is the same as quakes freeze tag) and clan arena. These modes also seem to contain a twist it appears you can pick up power ups that give you the ability to transform into demons, what type of game modes these power ups are included in or how many different types of power ups are available we are not quite sure yet but these may add a bit of a refreshing feel to game modes that are standard to FPS multi-player games like domination.

It seems multi-player does not end there ID software is also releasing a creation kit for Doom 4 called Doom SnapMap that is available for all platforms. Doom SnapMap allows you to create new maps, completely custom game modes/gameplay. While creation kits are nothing new, the potential for amazing creative maps and game modes seems far better than any other creation kit I have ever seen(on consoles that is) but the most interesting feature is the ability to edit game logic. What logic in the game is editable we still do not know but it still seems at the small glance we did get that this could allow gamers to create something truly special.
